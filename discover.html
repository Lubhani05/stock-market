Let's Learn Some basic terms in Stock market.

What is Stock Market?

        The stock market is a platform where buyers and sellers trade shares of publicly 
        listed companies. It provides a means for companies to raise capital and investors
        to buy and sell stocks, potentially generating returns through price appreciation 
        and dividends. It is influenced by various factors, including economic conditions
        and investor sentiment.

Stock Defination

        A stock represents ownership in a company and is issued in the form of shares.
        Investors who purchase stocks become shareholders and have a proportional claim
        on the company's assets, earnings, and voting rights. Stocks can be bought and
        sold on the stock market, offering potential financial gains or losses.

Share Defination

        A share represents ownership in a company. It is a unit of ownership that
        entitles the shareholder to a portion of the company's assets and profits.
        Shareholders typically have voting rights and may receive dividends based on
        their ownership percentage. Shares are bought and sold on stock exchanges.

what is stock exchanges?

       Stock exchanges are marketplaces where buyers and sellers trade stocks and 
       other securities. They provide a platform for companies to list their shares
       for public trading. Exchanges facilitate transparent and regulated transactions,
       enabling investors to buy and sell stocks at market-determined prices.
       Examples include the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the NASDAQ.

Stock Broker

       A broker is an intermediary facilitating financial transactions between buyers
       and sellers. For example, Charles Schwab is a brokerage firm that helps individuals 
       buy and sell stocks, bonds, and other securities on the stock market.

Demat and Trading Account

      Demat Account: A demat account, short for dematerialized account, is an electronic account 
      used to hold and store securities in a digital format, eliminating the need for physical
      share certificates. It allows investors to buy, sell, and hold stocks, bonds, mutual funds,
      and other securities electronically. 

      Trading Account: A trading account, on the other hand, is used to place buy or sell orders
      for securities on the stock market, facilitating the actual trading transactions. It is linked
      to the demat account for seamless transfer of securities.

Index

       An index in the stock market is a composite measurement that represents the performance of a
       specific group of stocks. Examples include the S&P 500, which tracks 500 large US companies,
       and in india Nifty and Sensex, which measures the performance of the top 50 companies in Nifty
       and 30 companies in sensex listed on the Stock Exchange.

Fundamental Analysis

      Fundamental analysis is a method of evaluating the intrinsic value of a 
      security by examining factors such as financial statements, industry trends,
      management quality, and economic indicators. It aims to assess the underlying
      fundamentals of a company or asset to determine its investment potential and
      make informed decisions.

Bull and Bear

       Bull:A bull market refers to a period of rising stock prices and optimistic investor sentiment,
       indicating a positive outlook. 

       Bear: a bear market refers to a period of falling stock prices and pessimistic investor sentiment,
       indicating a negative outlook.

Technical analysis

       Technical analysis is a method of evaluating financial markets by studying historical
       price and volume data. It involves analyzing charts and patterns to identify trends,
       support and resistance levels, and other indicators to predict future price movements
       and make investment decisions.

Indicators

          an indicator is a statistical tool or calculation used to analyze and predict market trends,
          patterns, and potential price movements. Examples include Moving averages, relative strength 
          index (RSI), and MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence), among others.

Moving Averages

          Moving averages are technical analysis tools that smooth out price data by calculating an average 
          over a specified period. They help identify trends and potential support and resistance levels by 
          reducing short-term price fluctuations, providing a clearer picture of the underlying price direction.
